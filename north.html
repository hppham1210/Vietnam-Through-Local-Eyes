Discover North Vietnam

North Vietnam is the historical and cultural foundation of the country. It is where Vietnam’s earliest civilizations developed, where dynasties ruled, and where the modern nation was shaped through centuries of resistance, change, and growth.

History and the Birth of Vietnam

Northern Vietnam has been the political center of the country for over a thousand years. In 1010, Emperor Ly Thai To moved the capital to Thang Long, which later became Hanoi. This marked the beginning of Hanoi as the heart of Vietnamese government, culture, and identity.

Over time, the north experienced periods of Chinese rule, Vietnamese dynasties, French colonization, and eventually revolutionary movements. In 1945, after the August Revolution, Hanoi became the capital of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.

During the Resistance War Against America to Save the Nation, North Vietnam played a central role in the fight for reunification. After 1975, the country was unified, and Hanoi became the capital of modern Vietnam. Today, the north still reflects layers of history through its architecture, culture, and daily life.

Daily Life and Culture

Life in North Vietnam starts early with coffee, street food, and markets. The streets quickly fill with motorbikes and local activity.

The north combines tradition and modern life. Temples, colonial buildings, and modern shops all exist together.

Nature and Landscapes

North Vietnam is known for limestone mountains, rivers, and rice fields. These landscapes create some of the most beautiful views in Vietnam.

Food Culture in the North

Northern food focuses on balance and freshness. It is less sweet and emphasizes natural flavors.

Why Visit the North?

North Vietnam offers history, culture, food, and nature all in one place. It gives visitors a deeper understanding of Vietnam.
